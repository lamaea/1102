Introduction

Conceptual Foundation

  • Autonomous Wealth Oversight: Leveraging digital environments to deliver financial guidance via algorithmic models, significantly reducing the necessity for human supervision [6].
  • Strategic Translation: Converting multifaceted user preferences and risk appetites into a structured, optimized portfolio through mathematical computation [9].
  • Market Inclusivity: A technological shift that democratizes high-level asset management by minimizing operational overhead and lowering entry thresholds for retail participants [2].

Primary Ambitions

  • Mitigating Cognitive Biases: Using data-driven discipline to override impulsive human emotions, preventing irrational behaviors such as market timing or panic selling [8].
  • Process Optimization: Enhancing the financial lifecycle by automating everything from initial profiling to continuous adjustment of asset weights [9].

Understanding Portfolio Construction

The core engine of a robo-advisory system is its portfolio construction algorithm. These algorithms translate a user's risk profile into a practical asset mix with measurable return and volatility targets.

1. Equal Weighting (1/N)

The Equal Weighting strategy assigns the same capital allocation to each asset regardless of historical return, volatility, or correlation.

  • Strengths: Highly intuitive, easy to implement, and requires no complex forecasting.
  • Weaknesses: It ignores risk differences across assets and can create an equity-dominated risk profile.

2. Risk Parity

Unlike equal weighting, Risk Parity allocates risk rather than capital, aiming for balanced volatility contribution across asset classes.

  • Strengths: Delivers stronger diversification across different macro environments.
  • Weaknesses: Often depends on leverage because more capital is allocated to lower-risk assets.

The Impact of Real-World Constraints

  • Transaction Costs: Frequent trading erodes returns and requires cost-aware optimization.
  • Rebalancing Frequency: Rebalancing must maintain target risk without creating excessive fees.
  • Asset Availability: Retail portfolios may rely on liquid ETFs as practical proxies for less accessible assets.

Our Models

In order to objectively evaluate the effectiveness of our algorithm, we conduct a comparative analysis of two asset allocation methods: Model A (Equal Weighting 1/N Model) as the benchmark, and Model B (Risk-Based Allocation Model) developed by our team.

This comparison highlights the difference between simplified heuristic allocation and personalized risk-based allocation in terms of expected annual returns and annual volatility.

1. Formula Design of Model A and Model B

Our model calculates expected annual return and annual volatility based on historical U.S. market data, including real interest-rate indicators and annual S&P 500 performance.

Basic Formula

Portfolio Return = (WEquities × REquities) + (WBonds × RBonds) + (WCash × RCash)

Portfolio Volatility = (WEquities × VEquities) + (WBonds × VBonds) + (WCash × VCash)

  • W: Asset weight in the portfolio. In Model A (1/N), W = 0.33 for each asset class.
  • V: Asset volatility used in portfolio risk estimation.
  • R: Asset return in the current year.

3. Comparative Analysis of Model A and Model B

3.1 Structural Difference

  • Model A: A stable benchmark model with equal fund distribution across assets.
  • Model B: A risk-based model that dynamically adjusts allocation percentages using historical data and risk signals.

3.2 Performance Difference

Expected Annual Return

  • In strong equity years (e.g., S&P 500 return of 28.71% in 2021), Model A may underperform because fixed weights limit upside participation.
  • Model B can identify high-return signals and adjust equity/bond/cash weights more effectively.

Annual Volatility

  • Model A cannot react to market stress due to fixed weights, which may amplify losses (e.g., S&P 500 return of -18.11% in 2022).
  • Model B reduces risk through dynamic weight adjustment under changing market conditions.

Conclusion

Model B provides better downside protection than Model A during volatile periods by incorporating real market indicators, such as the 10-Year Treasury Inflation-Indexed yield and the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX).

Our Discoveries

During the development of this robo-advisory system, we extended beyond implementation and examined how algorithmic models behave under real-world constraints such as volatility, investor psychology, and operational cost.

1. The Subjectivity Gap: Risk Profiling and Reality

  • The "Ideal" vs. The "Human": Financial capacity and emotional tolerance can conflict, even for users with strong long-term capacity [4].
  • The 2x Loss Aversion Law: Psychological pain from losses is approximately twice the pleasure of equivalent gains [4].
  • Our Discovery: A horizon-only model is insufficient; manual risk-tolerance controls improve behavioral suitability.

2. The Rebalancing Paradox: The Cost of Perfection

  • Optimal Frequency: More frequent rebalancing does not automatically generate better outcomes.
  • The 0.38% Net Alpha: Trigger-based rebalancing can outperform monthly rebalancing by reducing over-trading [7].
  • Our Discovery: Net-of-fee efficiency and controlled drift bands can reduce unnecessary tax and execution drag [5].

3. Transaction Costs: The Silent Profit Eroder

  • The Price of Friction: Repeated small trades can leak value through spreads and brokerage friction [1].
  • Compounding Effect: Minor per-trade costs can materially reduce long-term wealth outcomes [5].
  • Our Discovery: A cost-benefit execution filter can block low-value trades and protect long-term performance.

4. Asset Availability: Using Liquid ETFs as Proxies

  • The Institutional Barrier: Many alternative assets remain inaccessible to retail investors due to minimum size and illiquidity [3].
  • The Proxy Solution: Factor-mimicking ETFs can provide practical exposure to otherwise inaccessible themes.
  • Our Discovery: Liquid proxies support institutional-style diversification while preserving tradability and portfolio flexibility.
