Our Discoveries

When making this robo-advisor, we did not only implement it but also explored the behaviour of algorithmic models in a real-life situation by studying such variables as volatility, investor psychology and cost of operation.

1. The Subjectivity Gap: Risk Profiling and Reality.

  • ● The "Ideal" vs. The "Human" Financial capacity and emotional tolerance may be in conflict, even with long-term capacity users who have high long-term capacity [4].
  • ● The 2x Loss Aversion Law: It is discovered that the psychological pain as a result of losses is approximately twice the pleasure of equal gains [4].
  • ● A horizon-only model like our Discovery is not up to the task, behavioral suitability can be improved using manual risk-tolerance controls.

2. The Rebalancing Paradox: The Cost of Perfection.

  • ● Optimal Frequency: It is not always the case that higher frequency rebalancing yields better results.
  • ● The 0.38% Net Alpha: Net Alpha shows that Trigger-based rebalancing is superior to monthly rebalancing since it reduces over-trading [7].
  • ● We Find: Net-of-fee efficiency and controlled drift bands can be used to reduce the unnecessary drag and execution [5].

3. Transaction Costs: The Silent Profit Eroder.

  • ● The Price of Friction: Minor trades repeated can be a bleeder of spreads and friction by brokers [1].
  • ● Compounding Effect: The negative effects of a small amount of charges on per-trading can be drastic in the long-term in terms of wealth outcomes [5].
  • ● Our Discovery: A cost-benefit execution filter can filter low-value trades and guard long-term performance.

4. Availability of Assets: Proxying with Liquid ETFs.

  • ● The Institutional Barrier: Retail investors are not able to obtain most of the available alternative assets due to minimum size and insufficient liquidity [3].
  • ● The Proxy Solution: Factor-mimicking ETFs can provide an easy method to gain access to themes that are otherwise unavailable.
  • ● What we have discovered: Liquid proxies enable institutional-style diversification, and retain tradability and portfolio flexibility.
