Our Discoveries

In the construction of this robo-advisor, we went further than implementation and tested the behavior of algorithmic models in a real-life context by investigating variables like volatility, investor psychology and cost of operation.

1. The Subjectivity Gap: Risk Profiling and Reality

  • The "Ideal" vs. The "Human": Financial capacity and emotional tolerance may be in conflict, even with long-term capacity users who have high long-term capacity [4].
  • The 2x Loss Aversion Law: It is found that psychological pain caused by losses is about twice the pleasure of equal gains [4].
  • Our Discovery: A horizon-only model cannot do the job; behavioral suitability can be enhanced by manual risk-tolerance controls.

2. The Rebalancing Paradox: The Cost of Perfection

  • Optimal Frequency: It is not necessarily the case that more frequent rebalancing produces better results.
  • The 0.38% Net Alpha: Net Alpha indicates that trigger-based rebalancing is better than monthly rebalancing because it minimizes over-trading [7].
  • We Discover: The unnecessary tax and execution drag can be minimized using Net-of-fee efficiency and controlled drift bands [5].

3. Transaction Costs: The Silent Profit Eroder

  • The Price of Friction: Repeated minor trades may bleed value in spreads and brokerage friction [1].
  • Compounding Effect: Small amounts of per-trading charges can have a significant negative impact on wealth results in the long-term [5].
  • Our Discovery: A cost-benefit execution filter can filter low-value trades and guard long-term performance.

4. Asset Availability: Using Liquid ETFs as Proxies

  • The Institutional Barrier: Retail investors cannot access many alternative assets because of minimum size and lack of liquidity [3].
  • The Proxy Solution: Factor-mimicking ETFs may offer a convenient way to access themes that are otherwise unavailable.
  • Our Discovery: Liquid proxies allow institutional-style diversification, and retain tradability and portfolio flexibility.
