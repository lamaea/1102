Our Discoveries

During the development of this robo-advisory system, we extended beyond implementation and examined how algorithmic models behave under real-world constraints such as volatility, investor psychology, and operational cost.

1. The Subjectivity Gap: Risk Profiling and Reality

  • The "Ideal" vs. The "Human": Financial capacity and emotional tolerance can conflict, even for users with strong long-term capacity [4].
  • The 2x Loss Aversion Law: Psychological pain from losses is approximately twice the pleasure of equivalent gains [4].
  • Our Discovery: A horizon-only model is insufficient; manual risk-tolerance controls improve behavioral suitability.

2. The Rebalancing Paradox: The Cost of Perfection

  • Optimal Frequency: More frequent rebalancing does not automatically generate better outcomes.
  • The 0.38% Net Alpha: Trigger-based rebalancing can outperform monthly rebalancing by reducing over-trading [7].
  • Our Discovery: Net-of-fee efficiency and controlled drift bands can reduce unnecessary tax and execution drag [5].

3. Transaction Costs: The Silent Profit Eroder

  • The Price of Friction: Repeated small trades can leak value through spreads and brokerage friction [1].
  • Compounding Effect: Minor per-trade costs can materially reduce long-term wealth outcomes [5].
  • Our Discovery: A cost-benefit execution filter can block low-value trades and protect long-term performance.

4. Asset Availability: Using Liquid ETFs as Proxies

  • The Institutional Barrier: Many alternative assets remain inaccessible to retail investors due to minimum size and illiquidity [3].
  • The Proxy Solution: Factor-mimicking ETFs can provide practical exposure to otherwise inaccessible themes.
  • Our Discovery: Liquid proxies support institutional-style diversification while preserving tradability and portfolio flexibility.
