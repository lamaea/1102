How to use this tool

Adjust the questionnaire below to reflect your age, investment horizon, risk tolerance, and financial goal. Results and the chart update live; serve this site over a local HTTP server so ES modules load correctly.

Risk score methodology

The risk score is computed on a 0-100 scale from risk tolerance, goal, horizon, and age.

  • Tolerance: ((riskTolerance - 1) / 9) × 100
  • Goal prior: preservation -18, income -10, balanced 0, growth +10, aggressive +18
  • Horizon penalty: -35 × e^(-horizon / 6)
  • Age tilt: 8 × sin(((age - 18) / 67) × pi) - 2

behavioral = tolerance + goalPrior

rawScore = 0.35 × behavioral + 0.45 × (behavioral + horizonPenalty) + 0.15 × (behavioral + ageTilt) + 0.05 × (50 + ageTilt)

riskScore = round(clamp(rawScore, 0, 100))

Category: Conservative (<40), Moderate (40-64), Aggressive (>=65).

3.Comparative Analysis of Model A&B.

3.1.Structural Difference.

  • oModel A is a stable model, which distributes funds equally.
  • oModel B is a risk-based model, which uses the past database to dynamically adjust the investment percentage.

3.2.Performance Difference.

i.Expect Annual Return

  • oIn a good year for the stock market (such as the S&P 500 return of 28.71% in 2021), Model A may not be able to obtain excess returns due to fixed weights.
  • oModel B can allocate asset weights more effectively when high-return signals are identified through formula calculation.

ii.Annual Volatility.

  • oModel A cannot adjust when the market is bad due to the fixed weight, which may lead to losses (such as the S&P 500 return of -18.11% in 2022).
  • oModel B's weight adjustment can effectively reduce the risk.

iii.Conclusion

  • oModel B has better downward protection than Model A when the market fluctuates violently (such as the stock market fell by 18.11% in 2022) by reflecting "real data" (such as the Treasury Securities at 10-Year Constant Maturity and CBOE Volatility Index).
